## What disqualifies a fund

Bloom should NOT take money from:

- **Funds with thesis "AI for weddings"** — generic; will push toward consumer wedding tech.
- **Funds whose other portfolio includes a competing CRM** — conflict of interest.
- **Funds with strong board control demands at Series A** — too early for that level of constraint.
- **Funds without prior vertical-SaaS portfolio companies** — they don't understand the unit economics.

## When to kick off the process

Per `05-fund-returner-math.md`: the right Series A timing is **after** the first multi-venue customer signs (or has signed an LOI). Before then, valuation is constrained by traction.

Don't take a meeting "just to get feedback" — investors evaluate, and bad early impressions stick. Wait until the answers to their hard questions are in hand.

## Re-validation cadence

This checklist is a lifecycle, not a one-time list. Re-tick quarterly until Series A close. Items move from "in progress" to "done" to "outdated." A 12-month-old TAM/SAM/SOM is outdated; refresh.
