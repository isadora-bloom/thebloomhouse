The wedding-tech and adjacent landscape Bloom navigates. Each entry: what they do, what they're good at, what they can't do, where Bloom wins/loses.

## Direct CRM competitors (the customers Bloom imports from)

### HoneyBook

**Shape:** all-in-one CRM for service-based businesses (wedding venues + photographers + planners).
**Pricing:** $9-66/mo per seat.
**Customers:** [_____ count, source: ___]
**Strengths:** brand recognition, integrations (Stripe, QuickBooks), mobile UX.
**Weaknesses:** single-source data, no cross-source intelligence, AI is templated not learned.
**Bloom-vs-HoneyBook positioning:** "HoneyBook is your CRM. Bloom is your brain on top." Not a replacement; a layer.

### Aisle Planner

**Shape:** wedding-planner workflow tool (project management + planner-couple collaboration).
**Pricing:** [____, source ___]
**Customers:** [_____, mostly wedding planners (not venues)]
**Strengths:** workflow polish, planner-customer relationships.
**Weaknesses:** thin data layer, no AI, single-customer scope.
**Bloom-vs-AP:** different ICP. Bloom sells to venues, AP sells to planners. Not a direct collision.

### Tripleseat

**Shape:** event-venue CRM, primarily for restaurants and corporate event spaces. Some wedding venue users.
**Pricing:** mid-market, ~$300-1500/mo per venue.
**Strengths:** integrations with restaurant POS, deep BEO (banquet event order) workflow.
**Weaknesses:** not wedding-specific, AI thin, limited to single-venue typically.
**Bloom-vs-Tripleseat:** different vertical primary, but expansion-vertical territory. See `12-expansion-verticals.md`.

### Dubsado

**Shape:** small-business workflow tool, popular with wedding photographers and small planners.
**Strengths:** automation chains, customisable workflows.
**Weaknesses:** clunky UI, weak intelligence, shrinking market share.
**Bloom-vs-Dubsado:** different ICP. Mostly photographers, not venues.

### BridebookHQ

**Shape:** UK-focused wedding venue platform.
**Strengths:** dominant in UK directory market.
**Weaknesses:** not US-relevant yet.
**Bloom-vs-Bridebook:** future relevance if/when Bloom expands UK.

## Marketplace competitors (the data sources Bloom imports from)

### The Knot Worldwide (TKWW)

**Shape:** wedding marketplace + planning tools (Knot, WeddingWire, both owned by TKWW).
**Pricing:** vendor listings ~$200-2000/mo.
**Strengths:** consumer brand for couples; SEO dominance for "wedding venue near me"; lead-source platform venues pay for.
**Weaknesses:** their intelligence on venues is for their pricing/sales — not exposed to venues. They are a data SOURCE Bloom imports from, not a competitor.
**Bloom-vs-TKWW:** complementary. Bloom imports their leads and visitor activity.

### Zola

**Shape:** wedding registry + vendor marketplace.
**Strengths:** newer brand, registry stickiness.
**Weaknesses:** smaller venue presence than TKWW.
**Bloom-vs-Zola:** import-only relationship.

## Indirect competitors (DIY analysts)

### Spreadsheets + ChatGPT

**Many venues today:** export CSVs from Knot/HoneyBook, paste into a spreadsheet, ask ChatGPT for analysis.
**Their advantages:** free, customisable.
**Their disadvantages:** no automation, no voice DNA, no continuous learning, no draft generation.
**Bloom-vs-spreadsheets:** Bloom replaces this for venues that recognise the time cost. Doesn't replace it for venues that can't see beyond ChatGPT.

## Adjacent ecosystems

### Cvent

**Shape:** corporate event management platform.
**Relevance:** if Bloom expands into corporate-event-venue vertical, Cvent is the incumbent to displace OR partner with.

### OpenTable

**Shape:** restaurant reservation + analytics.
**Relevance:** if Bloom expands into restaurant-venue weddings (rehearsal dinners, smaller weddings), OpenTable's data layer is adjacent.

### Tave / 17hats / Sprout Studio

**Shape:** photographer-CRM tools.
**Relevance:** photographer is a different ICP. Possible future Bloom expansion target.
