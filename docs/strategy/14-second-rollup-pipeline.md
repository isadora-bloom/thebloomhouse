# Second roll-up pipeline

**Status:** TEMPLATE.
**Anchor:** `13-enterprise-pricing-wedgewood.md`.

Single-customer-concentration risk: if Bloom's only multi-venue customer is Wedgewood, that customer's pricing power eventually compresses Bloom's margin and Bloom's strategic options. The defense is a second roll-up customer of comparable size.

## Why this matters

Series A investors will ask: "What if Wedgewood walks?"

If the answer is "we have one other named multi-venue customer in conversation," the question stops there. If the answer is "we'd really struggle," the round is harder to close at the price.

A pipeline of 3-5 named candidates, with at least one in active sales conversation, is the defense.

## Candidate roll-ups in the wedding-venue market

### Tier 1: Established US wedding-venue collections

- **Wedgewood Weddings & Events** — primary prospect; ~70 venues; SoCal-based; well-known in industry.
- **The Davids Bridal Collection** — multi-venue collection but more vendor-shaped than venue-rollup. May or may not fit.
- **PWP Wedding Venues** (Premier Wedding Properties) — smaller US-based collection.
- **The Knot Worldwide's Editorial Venues** — TKWW has invested in some venue properties; long shot.

### Tier 2: Hospitality groups with wedding-venue arms

- **Marriott / Hilton / IHG event venues** — large hotel groups with wedding-venue arms. Different buyer (hotel ops vs wedding-specific) but possible.
- **PE-backed boutique hotel groups** — Brookfield, Blackstone, etc. own hotel collections that include wedding-capable properties.
- **Aspen Hospitality / Hyatt Inclusive** — destination-wedding properties.

### Tier 3: International + emerging

- **Bridebook UK + their associated venue collections** — UK-specific.
- **Wedding venue groups in Australia / NZ** — secondary markets, expansion phase.

## Outreach plan

### Quarterly cadence

Each quarter:
1. Re-rank the candidate list based on recent industry news (acquisitions, expansions, exec changes).
2. Identify warm-intro paths to top-3 prospects.
3. Send one founder-led outreach per top-3 prospect (Isadora's name + Rixey context + specific value proposition).
4. Track responses + advance any willing-to-talk to a discovery call.

### Discovery call template

A 30-min call with a roll-up's COO / VP Operations:

1. **5 min:** their pain — "Tell me about how a typical week looks for you across the venues. What's eating your time?"
2. **10 min:** show one specific brain output that they don't have today (cross-venue source quality benchmark; coordinator-time-savings extrapolated; portfolio-level risk dashboard).
3. **5 min:** acknowledge their alternatives — "You could build this internally, or wait for HoneyBook to ship something. Here's why neither solves it on your timeline."
4. **10 min:** discuss how a 60-day pilot at 3-5 venues would prove or disprove the value, and what the next step is.

NEVER pitch pricing on the first call.

## What "good" looks like

By Series A close (per `05-fund-returner-math.md`):
- 1 multi-venue customer signed (Tier 1 candidate)
- 2 multi-venue customers in active conversation
- Pipeline of 5+ named candidates with warm-intro paths

If only 1 prospect at Series A: too much customer concentration; investors will price down.

If 0 prospects at Series A: defer the raise or take a smaller round at lower valuation.

## Anti-pattern: chasing too many at once

Don't try to actively sell to 10 roll-ups simultaneously. Each one is a 6-12 month sales cycle. Bandwidth-allocate to the top 3 only. The rest stay in awareness mode (industry events, occasional check-in) until top-3 closes or stalls.
